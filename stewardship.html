Mission: Keep Dreams Alive
Why It Exists

Because too many young people are told, directly or indirectly, to expect less from life.

In too many at-risk communities, children grow up surrounded by limitations — limited resources, limited exposure, limited support, and sometimes limited belief in their own future. The Keeping Dreams Alive Foundation was built to push back against that reality.

Its mission has never been simply to help children get through the moment. It has been to help them see beyond the moment — to understand that discipline, guidance, encouragement, and opportunity can open doors they may never have imagined possible.

Why It Matters

A child does not need pity. A child needs belief, structure, and someone willing to show up.

That is what makes this work so important. A program can create confidence. A mentor can reshape identity. A team, a lesson, a meal, a conversation, or an opportunity can interrupt a cycle and replace it with hope.

For many children, being seen changes everything. Being believed in changes even more.

25 Years of Service

The difference is measured in changed lives, not headlines.

Over the past 25 years, the Keeping Dreams Alive Foundation has served more than 20,000 students and student-athletes across communities that needed real support, not empty words. Through mentoring, encouragement, educational support, life-development experiences, and consistent presence, the foundation has worked to help young people believe that their future can be bigger than their circumstances.

This is impact that compounds. When a young person grows in confidence, discipline, and self-worth, the effect reaches families, schools, teams, neighborhoods, and the next generation that follows.

Personal Meaning

This is not a side project. It is part of Glenn’s life story.

The foundation matters deeply because it reflects what success is supposed to do. It is not only meant to build businesses or create achievements. It is meant to create responsibility. It is meant to lift others. It is meant to leave something behind that is measured in human lives, not just professional results.

That is why stewardship belongs here. Because the heart behind Stoutt Property Management is the same heart behind Keeping Dreams Alive Foundation: lead well, serve well, and leave people better than you found them.

The Legacy

What matters most is not only the properties managed — it is the people helped along the way.

Buildings matter. Communities matter. Boards matter. But when all is said and done, the deepest legacy comes from investing in lives that are still being formed. That is where stewardship becomes personal.

Keeping Dreams Alive Foundation represents a promise that the work will always stand for something more than business alone. It stands for the idea that every child deserves a chance to dream, to be guided, and to discover that their life carries purpose.

The greatest thing we can build is not a company. It is hope in the life of a child.
